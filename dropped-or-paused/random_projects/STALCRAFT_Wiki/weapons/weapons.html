Weapons

STALCRAFT is a time-tested MMO shooter set in the open world of the Chernobyl Exclusion Zone. During your entire journey, you will be given a huge number of weapons to choose from, for every taste, and in this block you can consider any of them in more detail in a guide format.

Weapon classes

Each weapon belongs to a certain class, no matter how versatile or narrow it is. It is the weapon class that determines what unique bonus this weapon will receive and how it will be used, but the weapon class is not the only factor that determines this, for example, each pistol has its own bonus, but automatic pistols cannot have such a bonus, but even in this situation there are exceptions to the rule.

At the moment, there are 9 classes of weapons, each of which has unique characteristics when improved, perks and other features. It:

  • Melee
  • Pistol
  • Sub Machine Gun (SMG)
  • Assault Rifle (AR)
  • Machine Gun (MG)
  • Sniper Rifle
  • Shotgun
  • Other
  • Devices

Rank System

Rank. Initially, it may seem that it is needed only for the conditional division of weapons, but the rank also shows what characteristics it will have relative to other weapons, for example, a master rank weapon will be better than any weapon of the same class, but lower rank, the rank also affects the final GS of the weapon.

There are 6 ranks in total in the game, but only 5 are available for weapons, these are:

  1. Master Key
  2. Novice
  3. Stalker
  4. Veteran
  5. Master
  6. The Legend
The Legend rank is only available for the Armored Skeleton “Stormtrooper Albatross” and its variations.

Before the global overhaul, the equipment in the game did not have a ranking system, but a rarity system that could differ for one weapon several times, for example, the game had a unique Gauss cannon, which could only be obtained from a regular container before the overhaul.

Types of Access

Each weapon can be in two states:

  • Unique - such a weapon can be obtained in a personal version immediately after receiving it, it is impossible to transfer such a weapon. Basically, these are weapons for barter and rewards for quests and events.
  • Free - this weapon becomes personal only after use, so it can either be used and made personal, or transferred to another player, or sold at auction. Free weapons are all weapons from the donation store, with the exception of special sets, as well as weapons from barter for signal beacons. Also, a large number of weapons remained in the game that were free before the restructuring and can be found from other players, but this does not mean that now it can be obtained in a free version.
